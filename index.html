salty glitches

six archival prints of approx. 400 screenshots, qr-codes

salty glitches is the starting point of a counter-mapping project that uses google earth satellite imagery to problematize the notion of truth in digital media while drawing attention to zones of ecological disaster. the photographs explore the relationship between technology, people and nature as revealed through digital errors.

each color fragement that composes the salty glitches was located nearby the atacama desert and the jujuy region, both reservoirs of the precious mineral lithium. pipes and roads leading to drilling sites witness to the human interference that is taking place by a mega industry.
the qr-code is linking to the exact coordinates in google maps. in 2022, the bugs were fixed.

While meditation emerges as a popular coping strategy for inner healing and self-care in our digitized everyday lives, technologies heal too. Bugs are being fixed. Hopeful uncertainties vanish.

The 16-channel video installation 'stay a little longer, bug' follows the vivid existence of random digital phenomena. It searches for the qualities and visual ecstasies of unintended code issues. By slowly getting closer, it lets the bugs speak, show their inner being, and breathe.
